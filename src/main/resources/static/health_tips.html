Before Donating Blood

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water in the 24 hours leading up to your donation. Proper hydration helps maintain your blood volume and makes the donation process smoother.

Eat a Balanced Meal

Have a nutritious meal 2-3 hours before donating. Include iron-rich foods like spinach, red meat, or beans to ensure your hemoglobin levels are adequate.

Get Plenty of Rest

Ensure you get a good nightâ€™s sleep before your donation. Being well-rested helps your body handle the donation process better.

Avoid Alcohol and Caffeine

Refrain from consuming alcohol or excessive caffeine before donating, as they can dehydrate you and affect your blood pressure.

Check Your Eligibility

Ensure you meet the eligibility criteria, such as being in good health, meeting the minimum weight requirement, and not having donated recently (typically 56 days for whole blood).

After Donating Blood

Rehydrate Your Body

Drink plenty of fluids, such as water or juice, to replenish your blood volume. Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours after donation.

Eat Nutritious Foods

Consume a balanced meal rich in iron and vitamins to help your body recover and replenish red blood cells. Foods like leafy greens, eggs, and fruits are ideal.

Rest and Relax

Take it easy for the rest of the day. Avoid strenuous activities, heavy lifting, or intense exercise for at least 24 hours to allow your body to recover.

Keep the Bandage On

Leave the bandage on your arm for at least 4-5 hours to prevent bruising. Keep the area clean and dry to avoid infection.

Watch for Side Effects

Monitor for signs of dizziness, fatigue, or bruising. If you feel unwell or notice excessive bleeding, contact a healthcare provider immediately.
